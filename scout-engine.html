Vulnerable Populations in Decision-Making

SCOUT survey

Survey for community members—aligned to empowerment dimensions (WELI-style). Answer each question; SCOUT will produce indices, warning signs, and action prompts. Use as in the field (phone or tablet) or to see how the tool works.

Production decisions

When your household decides what to grow or which animals to keep, what is your role?

Nutrition

When your household decides how to spend money on food and nutrition, what is your role?

Resource control

When it comes to access to and control over land, water, or grazing, what is your role in your household?

Income control

When it comes to income from livestock, crops, or conservancy work, who controls it and what is your role?

Opportunities

Regarding groups or meetings that affect the conservancy and carbon project, which best describes you?

Time & workload

How much say do you have over your own time and the work you are expected to do?

Voice in meetings

In conservancy or carbon-project meetings, which best describes your ability to speak and be heard?

Process

1
Existing information extraction – Social CoMMs

Social CoMMs surveys act as an excellent baseline method to obtain data on household assets, water & nutrition, and perceptions on security, conservancy, wildlife, and financing impacts.

Ref: Social CoMMs: a guide to socio-economic monitoring in NRT member community conservancies (Deepali Gohil, Jessica Musengezi, NRT).

2
Targeting index-relevant questions towards marginalized groups

Modeled after existing proven measurements of women’s empowerment: decisions about agricultural production, access to and control over resources, access to and control of opportunities.

Aligned with Pro-WEAI (IFPRI) and WELI.

3
SCOUT: Social Conservancy Oversight & Utilization Tool

A revamped powerful tool for social impact measurement, incorporating views of traditionally less outspoken groups in conservancy communities. End results are a closer representation of household perceptions and can be utilized to inform both carbon market educational programs and future policy implementation.

Empowerment dimensions

SCOUT maps inputs to these dimensions (see ILRI WELI):

  1. Decisions about agricultural production
  2. Decisions related to nutrition
  3. Access to and control over resources
  4. Control and use of income
  5. Access to and control of opportunities
  6. Workload and control over own time
SDG 5: Gender Equality SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities

SCOUT outputs

  • Community-level indices: Inclusivity, Voice, Resource control, Trust in process
  • Warning signs: Sudden drops, Gender divergence, Repeated unresolved complaints
  • Action prompts: “Education module needed on X”, “Governance response overdue”, “Benefit-sharing dispute emerging”

SCOUT should force action, not just report.

Transparent carbon, accountable governance

CarbonWatch connects NRT conservancy communities to carbon credit flows through bidirectional communication, inclusive measurement, and public accountability.

Approach

Communication

Bidirectional channels so community members can feed perspectives and needs upstream into governance, and receive alerts, updates, and status on carbon and conservancy matters.

Inclusivity

Inclusive measurement using contestable, verifiable methods (e.g. Social CoMMs–style surveys) so marginalized and low-participation groups are represented in decision-making and indicators.

Education

Transparency across the carbon credit process so proceeds and transactions are understandable at all levels, and communities are informed and able to engage with climate finance.

How it works

Community feedback flows via SMS/IVR to implementing partners (e.g. AIID Africa, Wowzi). Data is managed for integrity and action; education on carbon is delivered through the same channels and reflected on the public dashboard.

  1. SMS / IVR — Community reports and surveys.
  2. SCOUT — Empowerment and governance indicators; flags and action prompts.
  3. Implementing agency — Response (logged).
  4. Education — Carbon modules via SMS/IVR; summarized on dashboard.
  5. Dashboard — Issue, response time, education, follow-up.

Empowerment dimensions

SCOUT maps community inputs to these dimensions (ILRI WELI).

  1. Decisions about agricultural production
  2. Decisions related to nutrition
  3. Access to and control over resources
  4. Control and use of income
  5. Access to and control of opportunities
  6. Workload and control over own time
